Katherine Johnson

Pioneering NESA Mathematician

Timeline of Katherine Johnson's life

1918 - Katherine Johnson is born in White Sulphur Springs, West Virginia.
1928 - Katherine enters West Virginia State College at age 10, graduating with degrees in mathematics and French at age 18.
1939 - Katherine becomes one of the first black women to desegregate West Virginia University's graduate school.
1940 - Katherine is hired as a “computer” at the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA) in Langley, Virginia.
1953 - Katherine joins a team of African-American women who calculate mathematical equations by hand at the Langley Research Center.
1961 - Katherine calculates the trajectories for the space flight of astronaut Alan Shepard, the first American in space.
1962 - Katherine checks the calculations of John Glenn’s orbit around Earth by hand, as Glenn insisted on her doing so.
1969 - Katherine calculates the trajectories for the Apollo 11 mission, which successfully lands astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin on the moon.
1986 - Katherine retires from NASA after 33 years of service.
2015 - Katherine is awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Barack Obama for her pioneering work in the space program.
2016 - Katherine's story is featured in the book “Hidden Figures” and the subsequent film adaptation, bringing her achievements to a wider audience.
2020 - Katherine Johnson passes away at the age of 101.
